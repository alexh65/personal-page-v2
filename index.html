A Somewhat Decent Bio

Hello there! My name is Nhu, but you can call me Alex. I was born in Vietnam 24 years ago. When I was 18 years old, I left everything behind in Vietnam to pursue higher education in the strange and exciting city of Vancouver.

After graduating with an Associate Degree from Langara College in 2019, I transferred to Simon Fraser University for their Software System program. I found myself especially interested in web development. After learning the basics of HTML, CSS and Javascript, I advanced to web frameworks like Flask, Django, and Vue for more complex web applications. I was able to score a full-stack software engineer internship with Greenspace Mental Health in 2021 which further improved my skills in a professional setting. I don't ever want to stop learning. I am looking for great opportunities that allow me to contribute positively to our community and promote my personal growth.

In my free time, I enjoy hiking, biking, and recycling. I'm also a movie buff. You can follow me on Letterboxd if you can find my account ;)

It was my first full-stack web developer position ever! The experience introduced me to many new frameworks and platforms I was unfamiliar with like Django and Contentful. I have prior experience with Bootstrap, but I finally learned to use it properly. I also got a better idea of a feature cycle from inception to deployment. The scrum team at Greenspace was one of the friendliest groups of people I've ever met. They taught me the best software development practices and boosted my confidence in a male-dominated field. Special shoutout to Mackenzie who was my onboarding buddy for my first month! I named my Chromecast after you!

For my first co-op semester, I was an instructor at Robokids, a STEM center based in Burnaby. I taught kids how to code in Python and Scratch. Plus, I learned how to use Tinkercad and SketchUp to teach 3D modelling classes. Teaching during the first year of COVID was hard. Everything was remote. I didn't receive any formal training as a teacher but learned by observing other instructors. The experience enhanced my ability to adapt to new situations and my leadership and interpersonal skills.

During the first month of COVID lockdown, I got my first job as a dietary aide at a long-term care home. It was a fast-paced work environment. I portioned and served food. We communicated with each other throughout the shift to ensure that every meal got delivered in time. I even got to operate a commercial dishwashing machine! I met people from different backgrounds; most of them were immigrants like me. I listened to their stories, and I could relate to them on so many levels. Although the experience was short-lived, it was rewarding, and I understood the hardship that a front-line worker had to go through during the worst month of the pandemic.
